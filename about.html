The Story

A Passion for Authentic
Indian Cuisine

The sigree — a traditional Indian charcoal brazier — has been at the heart of Indian cooking for centuries. Long before gas jets and induction hobs, skilled cooks knew that live fire produced something no other method could replicate: deep, smoky caramelisation, juicy interiors, and the unmistakable aroma of charcoal.

Sigree Indian Cuisine was founded on the belief that Auckland deserved a restaurant where those time-honoured techniques were practised seriously — not as a gimmick, but as craft. Our team of experienced chefs have roots across India, from the tandoor kitchens of Punjab to the coastal seafood traditions of Goa, bringing genuine expertise to every section of our menu.

Located in the heart of Avondale, we've built a place where families, couples, and groups can gather around flavours that feel both familiar and exciting — rooted in tradition, refined for today.

How We Cook

The Craft Behind Every Dish

Live Charcoal Fire

Everything we grill passes over authentic charcoal. The heat is patient and precise — producing that elusive crust and smokiness that only open fire can achieve. No shortcuts, no substitutes.

Layered Marinades

Our marinades are built in stages — tenderising first, then flavouring, then sealing. Proteins rest overnight so that every bite carries depth from the inside out, not just on the surface.

Regional Spice Craft

India's culinary landscape is vast. Our menu draws from multiple regions — the cream-rich gravies of the North, the aromatic coconut curries of the South, and the street-spiced chaat of the West.

Fresh Daily Prep

Sauces, breads, and spice blends are made fresh each day. Nothing is pre-made from a jar. Our kitchen starts early and works methodically so that every service is served at its peak.

Carefully Plated

We believe presentation honours the food. Each plate is assembled with attention to colour, balance, and portion — because the first bite is always with the eyes.

Warm, Attentive Service

Our team is trained to be present without being intrusive. We learn preferences, remember regulars, and make every guest feel genuinely welcomed — from the first greeting to the final farewell.
